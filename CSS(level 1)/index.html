The logo theory posits that the Avengers logo, specifically the circle around the "A," acts as a visual foreshadowing of the movie's outcome. A full circle around the "A" (as seen in The Avengers and Endgame) indicates the Avengers winning. A half circle (as seen in Infinity War) indicates the Avengers losing. The theory suggests this pattern will continue in future films like Avengers: Doomsday (full circle, victory) and Avengers: Secret Wars (half circle, loss)

Several characters in the Avengers universe have been the subject of fan theories and discussions regarding their bisexuality. While not explicitly stated in all cases, these characters are often cited as examples of potential or ambiguous LGBTQ+ representation. Loki, with his shapeshifting abilities and portrayal in Norse mythology, is often considered genderfluid and bisexual in comics and adaptations. Valkyrie, played by Tessa Thompson, is canonically bisexual in the MCU, with her sexuality alluded to in the films and confirmed by Thompson. Tony Stark's interactions with both men and women have led to fan theories of bisexuality, particularly in relation to his relationships with Steve Rogers and Doctor Strange.
